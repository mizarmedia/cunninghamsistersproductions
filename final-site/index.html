About the Film

Resilience is a non-profit narrative feature film that exposes the abuse, neglect, and exploitation occurring in unregulated teen treatment facilities across America.

Every year, thousands of teenagers are sent to wilderness programs, therapeutic boarding schools, and residential treatment centers. Many experience trauma that haunts them for life. This film tells their story.

The Movement

Why This Film Matters

120,000+ Minors currently held in congregate care facilities
$23B Annual revenue of the troubled teen industry
0 Federal agencies with oversight authority
1000+ Documented cases of abuse and neglect

The troubled teen industry operates with virtually no federal oversight. Programs that promise to help struggling youth often employ abusive tactics: isolation, physical restraint, denial of basic necessities, and psychological manipulation.

Resilience joins a growing movement of survivors, advocates, and filmmakers demanding change. From Paris Hilton's Congressional testimony to Netflix's "The Program," the truth is finally coming to light.
